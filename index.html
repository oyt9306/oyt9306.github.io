Industrial AI

Industrial AI : “Industrial IoT(IIOT) + Domain Knowledge + Artificial-intelligence(AI)”.
I conducted the research about industrial AI with the applications of industrial robots, planetary gearboxes, and power plant boiler. I predict the futures of industrrial AI can be categorized as following four parts.
First, Continual Learning for life-long network. To accomplish this, incremental learning, re-training strategies, and no catastrophic forgetting is required.
Second, Adaptive Learning for robust network. To accomplish this, scalable network, well on different working conditions, and domain-adaptive network is required.
Third, Physics-Informed Learning for guided network. To accomplish this, physics-consistent network, covering mechanical degradation, and regularized on each system is required.
Fourth, Self-Supervised Learning for intelligent network. To accomplish this, optimized network such as meta learning, auto labeling, and unlabeld data selection is required.

Industrial Robot

Robotic arms are widely used in various fields such as automotive, semiconductor, manufacturing, and medicine and so on. Because they are capable of various complex motions specialized in their tasks. Among them, especially, industrial robots are receiving lots of attention because they can withstand high torque loads and are specialized in repetitive motions like welding, assembly, painting, and inspection on the automated production line. However, when faults occur on industrial robots, a decrease in production performance could cause an unexpected shutdown of the production line which brings lots of economic losses. As operating robots of the fields become aged and exposed to potential danger which leads to the faults, fault detection of industrial robots is important.

Thermal Power Plant Big-Data

In recent years, leakage of the boiler tubes has been occurred frequently due to the degradation of aged tubes and over-operation to meet the increasing demands of domestic electric power. The leakage of the tube can be detected by using the acoustic signal acquired from the sensors attached outside of the boiler. We constructed a leakage detection algorithm by using the industrial time-series data acquired from an 800MW thermal power plant boiler. A correlation image based on a sliding window is generated and used as input data for deep learning. We developed the method for real-time diagnosis of the boiler states and leakage detection by distinguishing the shape of the correlation image through the convolutional neural network.
